Homecoming's here!

The tradition dates to the early '60s, when the school was still the Norfolk Division of the College of William & Mary. Homecoming was discontinued around 1990, but it came roaring back about a decade later. Recently added traditions include dyeing the Lion Fountain blue.

To get you in the spirit, we're including photos from recent and past Homecomings and highlighting new features for this year. You'll meet the marshal and hear reminiscences from alumni. We also have a schedule of events and a list of alumni who will receive awards.

See you soon!

For more information, go to www.odualumni.org

The costumes and hairstyles have changed. The fun hasn't. Here's a quick look back.

What's New This Year

New Homecoming events include a Friday night Alumni Webb Jam, Saturday morning yoga and Sunday brunch in downtown Norfolk (see schedule for more details).

Students won't be voting on a Homecoming king and queen; they'll be selecting two royals.

Family Weekend coincides with Homecoming. Monarch parents, join the fun!

The Marshal

Norfolk Mayor Kenny Alexander '90

His message: "I am looking forward to serving as ODU's Homecoming marshal and very much appreciate the honor. I'm always glad to join in opportunities that bring the university and the general community closer together. I hope my participation will symbolize our mutual desire to collaborate on many initiatives that benefit the entire city and region."

Savoring the moment: "Being invited to serve as Homecoming marshal also will help me complete another part of my own ODU experience. ODU did not have a football team when I was an undergraduate, so Homecoming wasn't as festive. Also, as a commuting student who worked in a family business, I did not have time to participate in a lot of campus activities."

Beyond Homecoming: "Students will encounter many opportunities as they continue their studies and grow as citizens. I came from very humble beginnings, I worked hard, I had fun, I participated in ways that I could, I always learned, and now I'm mayor of Norfolk. Be encouraged. Anything is possible. Thank you, ODU."

Read more about Norfolk Mayor Kenny Alexander '90 in the winter issue of Monarch magazine.

Alumni Reminiscences

Arlene Ingram '64 (M.S.Ed. '76): Then - "When I was a student, there wasn't a lot of pomp and circumstance. I don't think it was much of an event at all. There weren't a lot of alumni to bring back."

Now -- "It's a very appealing experience. We have a full day of activities. The parade is like nothing that I remembered. We never had a parade. There is a lot of hullabaloo and a lot of fun. The thing that impressed me the most one year was sitting in on a lecture by one of the professors in bioelectrics. It was a marvel to think that students have the opportunity to have this exposure, which was far beyond anything we had."

Michelle Smith '14

Behind the scenes - "I helped decorate and plan for Homecoming when I was a student. It's actually a lot of hard work, and it's hard to please everybody. But it's a lot of fun. Everybody had a really good vibe. It was like a family."

Her favorite float - "In the fall of 2012, my sorority, Pi Beta Phi, had a float that looked like a tent. It was a carnival theme. We had someone pretending to be a tiger jumping through a hoop and some blowup animals. We had two boys blowing fire. Pi Phi won the most spirited organization and first place in the Homecoming Parade competition that year. "

The experience as an alum: "I don't have to work it anymore. I can relax and enjoy it with my family. Sitting in the student section (during the football game) is awesome, but it's also awesome sitting on the 50-yard line with my parents."

Homecoming Schedule

THURSDAY

6 p.m. Alumni Honors Dinner, Norfolk Waterside Marriott.

FRIDAY

11 a.m. Jensen Communications Lab ribbon-cutting ceremony, Strome College of Business. The lab was financed by a generous gift from George Jensen '70.

11 a.m.-4 p.m. Relax & recharge with The Libraries, Learning Commons @ Perry Library. Light snacks provided. Special collections tours at 1 p.m. and 3 p.m.

1-4 p.m. Alumni Welcome Back Social, Barry M. Kornblau Alumni Center. Pick up ODU swag and your class button, meet Big Blue and mingle with other alums over light refreshments, beer and wine.

4 p.m. Dedication of Kate and John R. Broderick Dining Commons, 1401 W. 49th St. RSVP to http://odu.imodules.com/bco16 or 683-3116.

5-9 p.m. BAC Social, Baxter's, 500 Granby St. Guests pay for their own food. A portion of the proceeds will go to the Ronald Horne Scholarship.

10 p.m.-2 a.m. Alumni Webb Jam, Webb University Center. Snacks and cash bar available. Soundtrack by DJ Precise.

SATURDAY

8 a.m. Alumni Morning Yoga and Breakfast, Brock Commons, 47th Street and Monarch Way. Mats, water and light breakfast provided. Downward, Monarch!

10:30 a.m. Homecoming Parade, beginning at 49th Street and Powhatan Avenue.

Noon-7 p.m. NPHC Fraternities and Sororities Homecoming Reunion, Williamsburg Lawn.

12:30-3:30 p.m. Alumni Tailgate Tent, Kaufman Mall. Enjoy buffet, cash bar, games and raffles.

3:30 p.m. ODU vs. UTSA football game, Foreman Field. Go Monarchs!

SUNDAY

9:30 a.m.-2 p.m. Brunch, Freemason Abbey, 209 W. Freemason St. Specials include $3 mimosas, Bloody Marys, champagne and screwdrivers.
